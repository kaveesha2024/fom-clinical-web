About Us

The Lifestyle Counselling & Research Clinic will be involved in many activities related to lifestyle modifications to prevent and reverse chronic diseases. It will be established with the following objectives

Sri Lanka faces the triple burden of malnutrition, which includes undernutrition, overnutrition, and micronutrient deficiencies. The burden of malnutrition has been exacerbated by the current economic crisis and prevailing food insecurity. Despite the remarkable achievements in the overall health indices of the population, nutritional indicators have failed to keep up with improvements in health indicators. Underweight, stunting, and wasting, as well as poor micronutrient status in children under 5 years and women in the childbearing age group, continue to pose challenges to maternal and child health. This vulnerability can lead to poor growth and development, increased susceptibility to infections, suboptimal cognitive development, and reduced fertility.

The Lifestyle Counselling & Research Clinic will be involved in many activities related to lifestyle modifications to prevent and reverse chronic diseases. It will be established with the following objectives.

01

Assess and monitor the lifestyle of target groups at risk of non-communicable diseases (NCDs).

02

Evaluate food intake patterns, physical activity levels, sleep health, use of toxic substances, and environmental and mental health conditions.

03

Identify barriers and motivators for lifestyle changes.

04

Provide tailored lifestyle counseling and training for individuals needing support.

05

Design, implement, monitor, and evaluate the effectiveness of lifestyle interventions.

06

Promote local research to address gaps in the literature concerning chronic diseases and nutrition/lifestyle counseling.
